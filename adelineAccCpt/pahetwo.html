Education

Requirements

The requirements vary by location, but Ontarian candidates should have a bachelor's degree at an institution offering appropriate prerequisite courses in subject areas like Financial Reporting, Strategy and Governance Management, Accounting Audit and Assurance, Finance, Taxation. The courses must be completed within 10 years of the candidates PEP commencement.

  • GPA of 65% in core courses and 50% in non-core courses.
  • Candidates with relevant work experience may apply for an exemption from the currency requirement for a prerequisite course if they completed a relevant course more that 10 years prior to admission. Therefore a university degree isn't always necessary.

Accreditation Process

  • Students must complete CPA PEP modules that are not through their accredited program, in addition to the common final exam plus practical experience requirements:
  • Must complete CFE examination after this
    • Must also demonstrate breadth in all 6 technical competency areas (8 years of experience)
    • Must also complete 30 months of practical experience through a pre approved program or the experience verification route. Then provide recommendation letter from current employer or 30 month period employer, and have a resume that includes all work experience

Global Demand

Economic, industrial, and technological developments have resulted in a number of specialized fields in accounting; and the world will only continue to expand, resulting in CPAs being put in high demand. Businesses may also want CPAs because they can practice from anywhere in the world and may obtain global clients, building an international presence and reputation for the company they work for.
